Keaau, Orchidland & Hawaiian Acres catchment

Keaau sits right at the doorway between Hilo and Puna, and just behind town the land opens into the big agricultural subdivisions — Orchidland Estates and Hawaiian Acres — where lots run large and homes sit well off the road. Out on these parcels there's no county water; rain catchment is how families get water for the house, the garden, and the animals.

Those long lots and gravel roads don't slow us down — we're a quick run up from HPP, and we've serviced tanks all through the Orchidland and Hawaiian Acres grids. Whether you've got a newer poly tank or an older ferrocement or corrugated one, we bring the gear to handle it.

Why Keaau-area tanks need regular care

This corner of the island catches plenty of rain — well over 100 inches a year in the wetter pockets. That keeps your tank topped up, but it also carries roof grit, pollen, leaf litter, and VOG fallout straight into your catchment. Sediment settles on the floor, biofilm grows on the walls, and within a few years that buildup becomes prime territory for bacteria, even with filtration downstream.

Keeping the system clean means better-tasting water, longer filter life, and a pump that isn't straining against sludge. On a big Orchidland or Hawaiian Acres lot, dependable water is worth protecting.
