Volcano catchment has its own challenges

Up at Volcano's high elevation, the rain comes hard and often — your tank rarely runs low. But the same misty, forested setting that keeps you full also keeps your roof damp and shaded, which means moss, algae, and organic debris are constant companions. Everything washing off that roof ends up in your tank.

Then there's the VOG. When Kīlauea is active, volcanic gases and fine particulate drift across the village and settle on rooftops. Over time that acidic deposit can affect your roof surface and the quality of the water running into your catchment. Staying on top of roof washing and tank care isn't optional here — it's how you keep your water clean and your system healthy.

Why VOG and high rainfall demand extra care

  • VOG settles on your roof — volcanic deposits wash straight into the tank, so regular roof washing keeps that out of your water.
  • Acidic exposure — over years, VOG can be hard on roof surfaces and gutters; we catch problems before they cost you.
  • Constant moisture — damp, shaded roofs grow moss and algae fast, feeding sediment buildup below.
  • High-volume systems — heavy rainfall cycles a lot of water through, so filters and sanitizing need to stay current.

Common questions from Volcano homeowners

Does VOG really get into my catchment water?

It can. VOG settles on your roof and washes into the tank with the next rain. Regular roof washing and good multi-stage filtration are your best defense, and we help with both.

Do you service Volcano village regularly?

Yes — Volcano is part of our service area. We plan our routes so the elevation and distance are no obstacle to getting your system the care it needs.

How often should I wash my roof up here?

More often than most of the island. With constant moisture and VOG exposure, many Volcano homes benefit from roof washing on a regular schedule — we'll recommend the right interval for your roof.
