Pahoa & lower Puna run on catchment

Pahoa is the heart of lower Puna, and out here rain catchment is simply how homes get their water. Whether you're near the village, out in Leilani, Nanawale, or on an off-grid parcel down the highway, there's rarely a county water line — your roof and your tank do the work. We're only a short drive from Pahoa out of HPP, so getting to you isn't a problem.

Lower Puna properties range from tidy in-town lots to remote homesteads at the end of a long driveway. We've worked all of them, and we bring the right equipment whether your tank is a modern poly setup or an older corrugated one that's been collecting Puna rain for decades.

Why Pahoa-area tanks need regular care

Lower Puna catches some of the heaviest rain on the Big Island — easily 100-plus inches a year. That keeps tanks full, but it also flushes roof debris, pollen, and VOG fallout from Kīlauea straight into your catchment. Sediment piles up on the tank floor and biofilm builds on the walls, and over a few years that's exactly where bacteria take hold, even with filters running downstream.

Staying ahead of it means cleaner, better-tasting water, filters that last longer, and a pump that isn't fighting sludge. Out in the country, a reliable water system is one less thing to worry about.
