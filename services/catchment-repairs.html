Catchment problems we fix

  • Leaks & tank damage — a leaking tank, cracked fitting, or failing liner that's draining your water away.
  • Pump & pressure problems — weak flow, a pump that won't prime, short-cycling, or pressure that's dropped at the tap.
  • Stuck or failed filters — clogged sediment housings, a UV lamp that's quit, or filters that were never getting changed.
  • Contamination scares — cloudy, smelly, or off-tasting water, or critters and debris that got into the tank.
  • "No water" emergencies — you turned on the tap and nothing came out. We figure out why and get you flowing again.

Why catchment fails faster in Puna

Catchment systems in Hawaiian Paradise Park, Pahoa, and across Puna take a beating. With 120+ inches of rain a year, VOG off Kīlauea, and constant organic debris loading the tank, sediment clogs filters, pumps wear, and seals give out sooner than they would in a gentler climate. Small problems also escalate quickly here — a slow leak or a failing filter can become a no-water situation in days.

That's why fast response matters. The sooner we catch a problem, the smaller and cheaper the fix usually is — and the less time your ʻohana spends without water.
