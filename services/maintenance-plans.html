Why a plan makes sense in Puna

Catchment in Hawaiian Paradise Park, Pahoa, and across Puna needs steady upkeep. With 120+ inches of rain a year, VOG drifting off Kīlauea, and constant organic debris loading your system, filters clog and sediment builds faster than most people expect. Skip a filter change or two and you're drinking through a filter that's stopped working — and the problems compound from there.

A maintenance plan takes that worry off your plate. Instead of trying to remember when you last changed a filter, you have someone who knows your system showing up on schedule. For busy families, it's the simplest way to keep catchment water safe year-round.

Common questions

How often do filters really need changing?

For most Puna homes, every 3–6 months depending on your water and usage. Our heavy rain and organic load mean filters here work harder than the mainland norm, so we set your schedule around your actual system.

What if something breaks between visits?

Plan customers get priority scheduling and discounted repairs. You're first in line when something goes wrong, which is one of the biggest reasons families sign up.

Can I cancel or change my plan?

Of course. There's no pressure and no lock-in games — we want you on a plan because it genuinely helps, not because you're stuck. Adjust or pause it whenever your needs change.
